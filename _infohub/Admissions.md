<h5><strong>Admission to Year 3 of the Joint Integrated Programme at CHIJ SNGS</strong>&nbsp;</h5>
<p>CHIJ St. Nicholas Girls’ School welcomes outstanding Secondary 2 students
to apply for admission to Year 3 of the Joint Integrated Programme at CHIJ
SNGS. Successful applicants will spend 2 years in CHIJ SNGS and the final
2 years in Eunoia Junior College leading to the GCE ‘A’ Level Examinations.&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Selection Criteria</strong>
<br>Secondary 2 students who are keen to apply for Year 3 of the Joint Integrated
Programme at CHIJ SNGS must have:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>PSLE T-Score of 250 and above (excluding bonus points);(Students who do
not meet this criterion but performed exceptionally well in Secondary 1
and Secondary 2 might be considered)</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Excellent Secondary 1 and Secondary 2 academic results;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Active participation in co-curricular activities and community involvement;
&nbsp;and</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Obtained very good conduct grades in Secondary 1 and Secondary 2.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Shortlisted candidates will be invited to attend an interview at CHIJ
SNGS.&nbsp;</p>
<h5><strong>Admission for Returning Singaporeans</strong>&nbsp;</h5>
<p>Returning Singaporean children are Singapore Citizens or Singapore Permanent
Residents who currently reside overseas and are not studying in a mainstream
primary school in Singapore.&nbsp;</p>
<p>For further information on the available options to seek admission to
a mainstream secondary school in Singapore, including CHIJ SNGS, please
refer to the following website for details:&nbsp;</p>
<p>Admissions for Returning Singaporeans&nbsp;
<br><a href="https://www.moe.gov.sg/returning-singaporeans" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">https://www.moe.gov.sg/returning-singaporeans</a>
</p>
<h5><strong>Admission for International Students</strong>&nbsp;</h5>
<p>International Students who are keen to seek admission to a mainstream
school in Singapore, including CHIJ SNGS, are required to take part in
the Admissions Exercise for International Students (AEIS).&nbsp;</p>
